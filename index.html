    Spanish Omelette

    Though it sounds little fancy, due to its name, it isn’t totally different from any regular homemade Omelette!

    Nutrition Facts

    198 Calories; 12.9g protein; 14.2g of Fat(2 eggs)

    Following are the steps to prepare it in an easy way : -
    • 1st step is to break the egg (let’s say teo eggs) and put the egg insides in a bowl.
    • Then, add a pinch of salt (according to taste) (स्वाद अनुसार) in the bowl and stir it a little.
    • Then, add 1/2 portion of onion , finely chopped in the mix, along with 2 chopped green chilli.
    • Then, add 1/4 tablespoon chilli flakes and oregano seasoning in the mix .
    • Then, add 1 tablespoon mayonnaise ( can also add cheese instead) in the bowl an stir it for approx 1 minute. Make sure there are no lumps of mayonnaise left.
    • Then, heat a pan with 1 tablespoon sunflower/olive/mustard/soyabean oil and gently put the mix in the pan in medium flame.
    • Leave it for 1 minute, then change the flame to miniumum and cover the pan with a pan or any other thing and leave it for another 1 min.
    • Switch off the stove now! Your Spanish Omelette is ready!

    Paneer Stuffed Mushroom

    This dish a little time taking, but is worth it!

    • You need button mushrooms, paneer, cornflour and onions and green chillies in ingredients.
    Nutrition Facts

    21 Calories for 1 Stuffed Mushroom

    Following are the steps : -
    • Wash button mushrooms (say 250 g) and remove their stems.
    • Then, with a spoon, clear the inside of the mushroom canopy such that there is no trace of stem left.
    • Then, fill those empty canopies with grated paneer completely
    • Heat the butter in a broad non-stick pan, add the garlic and saute on a medium flame for a few seconds.
    • Place all the stuffed mushrooms in the pan, cover it with a lid and cook on a medium flame for 2 minutes.
    • Turn over the mushrooms gently and cook for another 2 to 3 minutes, or till all the water has been evaporated.
    • Your mushrooms are ready!

    Noodle Omelette

    This is simple to make! All you need to do is to make two things side by side.

    • You will be needing eggs, onions, green chillies, noodles(or even maggie!).
    Nutrition Facts

    414 Calories for 72g Noodles&Eggs

    Following are the steps : -
    • Boil noodles for 2 mins(or you can make maggie instead) and add instant masala and cook it, then take it out in a bowl, drain all the water.
    • Now, you need to make an omelette, so, crack 2 eggs and put in a bowl.
    • Chop 1/4 portion of an onion and 1 green chilli finely and put in the bowl.
    • Add salt (स्वाद अनुसार) in it and stir the mix well.
    • Now , heat a pan with 1 tablespoon oil and put the mix in it.
    • On minimum flame, spread the mix on the surface of pan to cover maximum area. This should be done as soon as possible!
    • Then, bring the noodles and place it above the omelette.
    • After 30 seconds, flip the omelette and press it from above and cook for 2 minutes.
    • Your noodles omelette is ready!

    Sweet Rice

    You must have came across the situation where the rice of previous day was still left and you were not willing to eat it! So, here’s a solution that might help! What about turning that rice into Kheer? Also, have you seen Sooryavansham?!!

    Nutrition Facts

    235 Calories per Serving

    So, let’s get started!
    • First, take a deep sauce pan and put 1 spoon ghee in it and start heating it.
    • Then, take 1/2 L full cream milk (for 2 person) and pour it in the pan and let the milk boil on minimum flame for about 10 minutes.
    • Then , put the rice which was left , in that pan and stir it continuously every 2 minutes.
    • Cook the rice till the milk and rice forms a semi liqud form and milk becomes creamy. Add sugar accordingly and turn off the stove.
    • Add cashews and raisins to enhance the taste!
    • Your Sweet Rice is ready!

    Fried Maggie

    We all love maggie, isn’t it! But sometimes good things also needs some changes sometimes!

    Nutrition Facts

    281 Calories per Serving

    Following are the steps to recreate this dish : -
    • Heat 2tbsp of oil in a pan.
    • Add finely chopped green chillies and let it fry for 2 min.
    • And finely chopped onions, fry till onions become translucent.
    • Add finely chopped tomatoes and Saute for few more seconds on medium high flame and then add some salt to it.
    • Now add one table spoon of soya sauce and mix it, then add Maggi taste maker which comes with its packet.
    • Add boiled vegetables to it, and cook them for 2 to 3 min.
    • You can add veggies of your choice and keep the mixture aside.
    • Now take a bowl, add 2-1/2 cup water and bring it to boil.
    • Add magggi, Stir for few seconds and then simmer the flame.
    • Leave to cook for about 3-4 minutes until noodles become soft and little dry.
    • Strain the water, keep the maggi aside.
    • Now, add the maggi to the vegetable mixture and mix them properly with the help of forks so that the the taste makers are mixed with maggi properly.
    • Your fried maggie is read!

    Egg Fried Rice

    Another good way to fancy the leftover rice is to fry it with vegetables and if you want, you can add eggs too!

    Nutrition Facts

    191 Calories

    So, let’s get started!
    • Crack 3 eggs into a small bowl and beat them together. Set the bowl aside.
    • Heat a wok (or large saute pan) with 1/2 tablespoon of the oil over medium-high heat. Once the pan is hot, add the beaten eggs and scramble them for about a minute. Transfer the eggs to a dish and turn off the heat. Wipe off the wok with a kitchen towel.
    • Drizzle the remaining 1 1/2 tablespoons of oil in the wok over medium-high heat. Add the onions and cook them for about 2 minutes, stirring constantly. Add the mixed vegetables and scallions and cook for another minute. I like to save some of the scallions (the dark green part) for garnish.
    • Add the cooked rice into the pan or wok and cook for a few minutes, until the rice is heated through. If you have large clumps of rice, break them apart with the back of your spatula.
    • Add the soy sauce, sesame oil, and five-spice powder (if using) and stir to distribute the sauces. Add the 3 scrambled eggs and stir to mix again. Serve immediately.
    • Enjoy your fried rice!!

    Soya Nuggets

    Are you a vegetarian ? But want to taste chicken.?? Or , are you a non-vegetariab but chicken is not available!!
    We have one solution for both!! Try soya Chunks!!

    Nutrition Facts

    345 Calories per Serving(100g)

    Following are the steps : -
    • Place the soya chunks in a bowl and fill it up with enough hot water so that the soya chunks are completely immersed. Leave it aside for 20 minutes.(you can instead boil them for 5 mins!).
    • After soaking in water, the soya chunks would appear double in volume. Squeeze all the water from the soya chunks. I prefer to cut soya chunk pieces into smaller ones.
    • Marinate it with pepper powder, chilli powder, ginger(crushed), garlic(crushed) and salt for 10 minutes.
    • Heat oil in pan/kadai. Add onions, curry leaves , finely chopped cabage and some salt. Salt absorbs moisture from onion and helps onion to get cooked fast. Saute it well until onions turn light brown.
    • Add turmeric powder, coriander powder and garam masala. Fry it well
    • Add tomato and mix it well. Cover and cook for 5 minutes
    • Add marinated soya and 1 cup water. Adjust salt. Cover and cook it for 15 minutes.
    • Now open the lid and cook until the water evaporates.
    • Enjoy!

    Cilantro Omelette

    This is just a fancy name for an omelette with coriander leaves as the second main ingredient , first is obviously eggs!

    Nutrition Facts

    200 Calories(2 Eggs)

    So, let’s get started!
    • Season the beaten eggs (lets’s say two eggs) well with salt and pepper and very fine paste of coriander leaves(say one small bowl) and mix till a homogenous mixture is formed.
    • Heat the oil and butter in a pan over a medium-low heat until the butter has melted and is foaming.
    • Pour the eggs into the pan, tilt the pan ever so slightly from one side to another to allow the eggs to swirl and cover the surface of the pan completely.
    • Aftr 30 seconds , fill the omelette with fresh spinach and coriander , finely chopped , and leave it for 1 minute.
    • Scatter the filling over the top of the omelette and fold gently in half with the Slide onto a plate to serve.
    • Your omelette is ready!!

    Some Indian Food Facts

    Origin of Chicken Tikka Masala

    Chicken tikka masala, the hugely popular Indian curry, is NOT Indian. It was invented in Glasgow, Scotland.

    Due to its unrivalled popularity, it has become synonymous with authentic Indian cuisine across the UK, despite having very little connection to the country itself. So, the next time you order a Tikka Masala, just remember, it may taste amazing, but it’s not truly authentic.

    Daal Bhaat or Daal Chaval is not INDIAN

    The most eaten dinner dish of India Daal Chawal. Daal Chawal is actually said to have originated in Nepal.

    Ghost Chilli

    India is home to bhot jolokia, one of the hottest chillies in the world. Also called “ghost chilli”, it is grown in North East states and is more than 400 times hotter than Tabasco sauce.

    Gulab Jaamun is not INDIAN

    If you think there can be nothing more Indian than Gulab Jamun, you are in for a shock. Deep fried balls of dough that were dipped in sugar syrup existed in the Mediterranean region by the name of Luqmat al Qadi, much before they came to India.
